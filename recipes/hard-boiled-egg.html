Hard-Boiled Egg

Description

A hard-boiled egg is a cooked egg where both the white and yolk are fully set, typically achieved by boiling the egg in water for about 9-12 minutes. The shell is then removed to reveal a firm, pale white egg white and a solid, golden yellow yolk. This simple, nutritious food is rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals, making it a popular snack or addition to salads, sandwiches, and various dishes. Hard-boiled eggs can be prepared in advance and stored in the refrigerator for convenience, offering a quick and satisfying option for any meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • Â¼ cup distilled white vinegar
  • 6 cups water
  • 8 large eggs

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Combine salt, vinegar, and water in a large pot, and bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Add eggs one at a time, being careful not to crack them. Reduce the heat to a gentle boil, and cook for 14 minutes.
  4. Once eggs have cooked, remove them from the hot water, and place into a container of ice water or cold, running water. Cool completely, about 15 minutes. Store in the refrigerator up to 1 week.
  5. Enjoy!
